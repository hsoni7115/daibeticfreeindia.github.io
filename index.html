Understanding Diabetes in India

Welcome to Our Diabetes Awareness Initiative

India is facing a growing health challenge, and it's time to address it head-on. Diabetes is becoming increasingly prevalent, affecting millions of lives across the country. This webpage is dedicated to spreading awareness about diabetes in India and empowering individuals with the knowledge they need to make informed choices for a healthier life.

What is Diabetes?


Diabetes is a chronic condition that affects the body's ability to process blood sugar (glucose). Insulin is a hormone that regulates blood glucose. Hyperglycaemia, also called raised blood glucose or raised blood sugar, is a common effect of uncontrolled diabetes and over time leads to serious damage to many of the body's systems, especially the nerves and blood vessels.
The two main types are Type 1 and Type 2:

Type 1 Diabetes:Typically diagnosed in childhood or adolescence.The body does not produce insulin, requiring regular insulin injections.

Type 2 Diabetes:More common in adults, but increasingly affecting younger populations. The body does not use insulin properly, leading to insulin resistance.

Risk Factors

Common risk factors for diabetes in India include:

1.Sedentary lifestyle

2.Unhealthy diet, high in sugary and fatty foods

3.Genetic predisposition

4.Obesity

5.Lack of awareness and regular health check-ups

Prevention and Management

Preventive Measures:

1.Adopt a healthy diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains.

2.Engage in regular physical activity.

3.Maintain a healthy weight.

Managing Diabetes:

1.Regular monitoring of blood sugar levels

2.Medication or insulin as prescribed by healthcare professionals.

3.Lifestyle modifications, including diet and exercise.

Our Message

This webpage is a part of a larger initiative to educate and empower individuals across India to take control of their health.
Through awareness campaigns, resources, and community support, we aim to reduce the impact of diabetes on our nation.
